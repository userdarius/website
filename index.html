About

I'm Darius, a computer science student at EPFL currently working on CoT reasoning and dataset optimization for llms. I'm documenting my learning notes and thoughts in this barely populated blog: dariusfoodeei.com.

I like to think about the future and build things. I'll also be documenting some projects I work on here! Some of my interests include:

  • Deep Learning and Reinforcement Learning These are fields I find very exciting. I believe the future of real world AI applied to industry and society will be game changing (some cool companies working on this include phaidra.ai and others) and I also think the economic impacts of self improving control systems in industry will be huge. I also think AGI will require a lot more than just scaling LLMs to become more autonomous and creative. "There are many problems in the world that are far too complex to hand-code solutions for. I expect this to be a large branch of machine learning, and an important step on the road towards general intelligence." - Sam Altman (I don't agree on keeping ai researched closed though)
  • Decentralized Systems and Ethereum I've worked on this for a few years now and I think it's a very interesting technology with huge potential, however I'm not sure how much of a difference it will make in the short to medium term. I think we'll need a lot more innovation in web3 (and a major shift in mindset) to make it more than just a speculative internet.
  • Robotics I think humanoid robotics will be the next big thing to solve the labor shortages in the world. I've worked personally on a few smaller projects like quadrupeds and robotic arms but nothing close to terminators.
  • Brain Computer Interfaces (here's a link to a cool intro) I'm not a neuroscientist but I believe BCI is a game changer for people with disabilities and will help us keep up with AGI and superintelligence once they become a reality.

In my past life, I cofounded a decentralized money market protocol called Honey. I've worked for Trader Joe and Gelato Network. I've dabbled in Web3 and decentralized systems for a few years as a developer after getting roped in by friends during my bachelor's, but have had trouble seeing the impact of my work on the world. I still think blockchain has a huge potential in finance and I own some bitcoin, but it's not something I want to work on in the long run by lack of interest in financial engineering (I'm not too convinced by the other supposed benefits of blockchain at least in their current form). I'll gladly talk about it if you want to though.

I've also done some work in high school in astrophysics. Here is a link to a paper I wrote on gravitational lensing and dark matter.
